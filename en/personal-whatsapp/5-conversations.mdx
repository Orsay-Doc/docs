Once your sequence is live, every lead that engages appears in **Conversations**.

## Follow conversations in Orsay

In the **Conversations** page you see every active thread, classified into four buckets the AI updates as the conversation progresses:

- **Positive** — booked or about to book
- **Engaged** — talking but not yet qualified
- **Negative** — not interested
- **Undetermined** — no signal yet

You can filter by classification, sort by newest/oldest, search, and click any thread to see the full message history. Re-classify manually if the AI gets it wrong — your correction also helps the AI improve via [Conversation Improvements](/en/overview/conversation-improvements).

## Take over a conversation — two ways

When you want to step in personally:

### From Orsay

Open the thread in **Conversations** and reply directly. You can:
- Type a text reply.
- Send a voice note (record in the input bar).
- Use **Text-to-Audio** to convert text into your cloned voice. → [Voice cloning](/en/guides&tutorials/voice-cloning)

When you reply, the AI agent **disables itself** for that thread automatically. Re-enable it with the toggle when you're done.

### From your phone (Personal WhatsApp only)

This is unique to Personal WhatsApp: because Orsay is connected to your real WhatsApp via QR code, **the conversation is also on your phone**. Open WhatsApp on your phone, find the chat, and reply normally.

When you send a message from your phone, the AI agent in Orsay **also disables itself** for that thread — Orsay sees your reply and steps back, so you and the agent never talk over each other.

<Tip>
Use phone takeover for high-value calls or when you're already chatting with the lead in person. Use Orsay takeover when you're at your desk and want to send a voice note in your cloned voice.
</Tip>
